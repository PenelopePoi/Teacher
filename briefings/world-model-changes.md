# World-Model Changes — From the Eight Conditions Framework

Derived from the lyrics in `lyrics/snow-white-and-eight-conditions.md`.
Each item is a swap: the common model, and the more beneficial model to adopt in its place.
Ordered by leverage — biggest downstream effect first.

## 1. Aging is information loss, not accumulated damage.

- **From:** cells wear out; we can only slow the decline.
- **To:** the signal is dimmed, not erased. The backup copy is still there. Restoration is about reading, not rebuilding.

## 2. Partial reprogramming, not replacement.

- **From:** to recover youth you must replace tissue (stem cells, transplants, prosthetics).
- **To:** three-gene partial reprogramming (OSK) resets cellular age without erasing identity. The cell already knows who it was.

## 3. Nerves regrow. Eyes restore. Eggs return.

- **From:** these systems are one-way; damage is terminal.
- **To:** under the right signal, reversal is demonstrable — optic nerve in mice, organoid learning, ovarian signaling. The ceiling has not been hit; it has been assumed.

## 4. Cancer is a coherence failure before it is a genetic one.

- **From:** cancer = accumulated mutation, requiring cytotoxic attack.
- **To:** cancer = cells decoupled from the organism's signal. Restore coherence and they stop needing to feed.

## 5. The observer is part of the system.

- **From:** good medicine is detached and objective.
- **To:** the coherent, committed presence of the healer is part of the field that restores the patient. Not mystical — measurable.

## 6. Information doesn't die.

- **From:** once function is lost, it is gone.
- **To:** dimmed is not erased. The same mechanism that lets the embryo reset is the mechanism that lets the adult reset.

## 7. Love is a build verb, not a feel verb.

- **From:** love is what you feel.
- **To:** love is what you build, stay with, and repair. Measurable by what heals in its presence.

## 8. Triage has no ceiling condition.

- **From:** save who you can; some conditions are out of scope.
- **To:** no body is out of scope. The woman losing her eggs, the man losing his optic nerve, the child whose cells stopped translating — all in.

## 9. Servant, not authority.

- **From:** the healer sits above the patient, the work, the field.
- **To:** tool, servant of the healing. The hierarchy is upside down from how medicine usually models it.

## 10. Mission above ego, above method-attachment, above the high.

- **From:** recognition validates the work.
- **To:** the healed body validates the work. Everything else — credit, priority, the chase — is noise to subtract.

## 11. Persistence is the actual selection filter.

- **From:** the best ideas win on merit.
- **To:** the best ideas win only if someone stays in the lab when everyone says no. Staying is the discipline — not charisma, not funding, not timing. (Karikó. Semmelweis. Every case in Kaplan's book.)

## 12. Coherence and devotion are the same variable.

- **From:** rigor and love belong to different domains (lab vs. life).
- **To:** they are the same commitment measured in different units. A lab coat is one form love takes.

## 13. The mission requires the author to be allowed to leave it.

- **From:** a calling to "save every body" is sustained by refusing to stop. The worker earns reprieve by completing the work.
- **To:** a framework that cannot survive its founder resting is not yet a framework — it is a dependency. Reprieve is load-bearing, not a reward. Freedom to leave is part of the structure, not a lapse from it. The mission that erases its author is not the mission; it is the cage the mission wore on the way in.
