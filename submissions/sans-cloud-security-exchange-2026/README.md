## Speaker bio template

Each submission includes a placeholder bio. Fill in the bracketed fields and trim to 100–150 words. Third person; no marketing language.

```
[Speaker name] is [current role] at [organisation], where [they] [one
sentence on what they do day-to-day relevant to cloud / AI security].
[They] previously [one sentence on prior experience that establishes
practitioner credibility — incidents handled, programs built, scale,
regulated context]. [Their] work focuses on [two or three concrete
areas drawn from the talk's topic]. [Optional: one sentence on
publications, OSS work, prior speaking, certifications if directly
relevant — skip if it would push past 150 words]. [They] are based in
[city] and can be reached at [contact handle / email].
```

A worked example (delete before submitting):

> Jane Doe is a Cloud Security Engineer at Example District IT, where she leads EDR triage and AI-tooling integration for a 1,000-endpoint K-12 fleet. She previously ran detection engineering at a regional MSSP, deconflicting AI-coding-agent activity from attacker living-off-the-land across Linux developer hosts. Her work focuses on telemetry-driven investigation, AI-tool noise suppression, and procurement frameworks for cloud-native components in regulated environments. She is based in Sacramento, CA.

## Submission checklist (per session)

- [ ] Title finalised (under 70 chars; descriptive, not clever)
- [ ] Speaker bio filled in, third person, 100–150 words
- [ ] Abstract reviewed — specific, problem/approach/takeaways, no marketing
- [ ] Outline timings sum to 30 minutes including Q&A
- [ ] Three to five concrete attendee takeaways listed
- [ ] No product names, no vendor pitches, no certifications-as-content
- [ ] Optional 60–90s overview video recorded (per CFP)
- [ ] Confirmed in-person availability (per CFP, in-person speakers prioritised)

## Speaker commitments to acknowledge before submitting

Per the CFP, selection grants SANS permission to use the speaker's name, photo, title, biography, and abstract for marketing; the session will be recorded and posted (YouTube + SANS Summit Access page); the speaker agrees to actively promote the session, submit final slides by deadline, and join an optional AV check.
